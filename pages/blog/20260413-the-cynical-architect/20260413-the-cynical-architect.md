_**Mayer's Law of Semantic Decay**_: Adoption and meaning always move in opposite directions.

A glossary of terms that meant something once — or are heading that way.

## New Problems With Old Names
_1990s–2000s_

> **API** — _We need two systems to talk._\
> **Web Services** — _They talk, now over the internet._\
> **Web 2.0** — _Still the web, but it reacts to you._\
> **Cloud** — _Let’s use someone else’s servers._

## Old Problems With New Names
_2010s_

> **Full Stack** — _One person builds the whole thing now._\
> **Microservices** — _Still services, but smaller (if we’re lucky)._\
> **Serverless** — _Still servers, but now someone else’s problem._\
> **Big Data** — _More data than we can possibly use._

## All Problems Now Solved With AI
_2020s–2030s_

> **AI** — _Still AI, but now it has a prompt._\
> **RAG** — _AI can finally use all that data (if we’re lucky)._\
> **MCP** — _AI can finally use APIs (if we’re lucky)._\
> **AI Agents** — _Loop the prompt and give it a job title._\
> **Human in the Loop** — _One person approves the whole thing now._\
> **Autonomous AI** — _AI loops itself, but now someone else’s problem._\
> **Sovereign AI** — _Let’s return to using our own servers._\
> **Embodied AI** — _All of the above, but now with legs._
